High School Mathematics


The Ontario Math Curriculum offers three University level courses: MHF4U (Advanced Functions), MCV4U (Calculus and Vectors), MDM4U (Data Management). These courses develop studentsâ€™ skills in calculus, linear algebra, and statistics that they will eventually need in university. For most university programs that are related to the sciences, business, mathematics, and engineering, they require students to take advanced functions and calculus in grade 12. Out of the three math courses, calculus and vectors is the most challenging course; it is essentially the combination of two different types of mathematics. Data management is the easiest course out of the three; however, it helps students consolidate their understanding of basic statistics in preparation for university.
